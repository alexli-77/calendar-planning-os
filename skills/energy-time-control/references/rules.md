# Energy Time Control Rules

These rules are distilled from time-management and brain-energy notes. Keep them advisory unless the user's calendar policy makes them mandatory.

## Day Shape

- Morning is usually the best place for deep work, writing, coding, hard thinking, and study.
- The first working block should avoid email and low-value admin when important work exists.
- Early afternoon is better for admin, meetings, errands, coordination, and lower-focus tasks.
- Late afternoon can be used for short deadline-driven work, review, or closing loops.
- Evening should usually be lighter: review, planning, learning, recovery, or personal time.

## Block Length

- Use 15 minutes for short, crisp actions.
- Use 45 minutes for normal focus blocks.
- Use 90 minutes for demanding deep work when the task is clear.
- Add a 15-30 minute buffer after deep work when possible.
- If a task needs more than 90 minutes, split it into separate blocks with a visible checkpoint.

## Recovery

- Do not treat meals, sleep, exercise, and rest as leftover time.
- Keep lunch and light movement as recovery anchors when the user configures them.
- A short nap or low-stimulation break can be better than forcing low-quality work.
- Avoid scheduling hard deep work late at night unless the user explicitly prefers it.

## Task Placement

- Put P0/P1 clear deep work in high-energy windows.
- Put email, reimbursements, form filling, and Linear/GitHub updates in admin windows.
- Put daily review, weekly review, and planning in review windows.
- Put exercise or personal routines in recovery windows.
- If the calendar is crowded, warn instead of filling every open slot.

## Output To Calendar Decision Layer

Return:

- recommended block type,
- preferred time window,
- suggested duration,
- whether a buffer is needed,
- warnings when the plan risks overload or poor energy fit.
